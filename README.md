## The Backup Strategy Nobody Talks About Enough

Here's something that doesn't get emphasized enough: your hardware wallet is only as secure as your backup strategy.

During setup, Trezor generates a recovery seedâ€”either 12, 20, or 24 words depending on what you choose. This seed is your master key. If you lose your device, this seed lets you recover everything on a new Trezor. But if someone else gets your seed, they own your crypto. Simple as that.

Trezor supports SLIP39 (Shamir Secret Sharing), which lets you split your seed into multiple shares. You might create 5 shares and require any 3 to recover. This means you can store shares in different locationsâ€”one at home, one in a safe deposit box, one with a trusted family memberâ€”and even if someone finds one or two shares, they can't access your wallet.

For additional security, you can enable a passphrase (sometimes called the "25th word"). This creates a hidden wallet that's separate from your standard wallet. Even if someone gets your recovery seed, they still can't access your passphrase-protected wallet without that additional word. Just don't forget your passphrase, because Trezor can't help you recover it.

## The Security Features That Actually Matter

Beyond the marketing fluff, here are the security features that genuinely make a difference:

**Offline Private Keys**: Your private keys never leave the device. Ever. Even when you connect to your computer, the keys stay isolated in the secure chip.

**Trusted Display**: Transaction details always appear on the device screen, not just on your computer. If your computer is compromised, you'll see the discrepancy on the Trezor screen.

**PIN Protection**: Set a PIN up to 50 digits long. After several wrong attempts, the device adds increasing delays between tries, making brute-force attacks impractical.

**Passphrase Protection**: Create hidden wallets that don't appear unless you enter the correct passphrase. Great for plausible deniability scenarios.

**Firmware Verification**: Every firmware update is cryptographically signed. The device won't install unsigned or tampered firmware.

**Open-Source Code**: Security researchers worldwide can audit the code. Vulnerabilities get found and fixed quickly.

**Shamir Backup**: Split your recovery seed into multiple shares for distributed storage.
